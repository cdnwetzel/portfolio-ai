## The Post

This Thanksgiving, I'm grateful for the outages.

Not because I enjoy 3 AM pages.
Not because "it's good experience."

Because nothing teaches faster than production on fire.

Every time I've grown the most:
→ A config change that took down payments
→ A "simple" migration that wasn't
→ The deployment that worked in staging (of course)

These moments taught me what no course, book, or certification ever could.

The engineer I am today was forged in incidents I never want to repeat.

So this week, I'm thankful for:
- The on-call rotations that built my instincts
- The teammates who answered at 2 AM
- The postmortems that made us better

**What's the incident that taught you the most?**

I'll share mine in the comments.

Happy Thanksgiving to everyone keeping the internet running this holiday. 🦃
